Abstract

This paper presents the design and development of a solar-powered eco-friendly cooking stove that incorporates PWM-controlled airflow for achieving smokeless combustion. The system leverages renewable solar energy to power a fan mechanism regulated by a PWM controller, enabling precise control over airflow and combustion efficiency. The proposed design significantly reduces smoke emissions compared to traditional biomass stoves, making it suitable for use in rural and underserved communities where clean cooking solutions are lacking.

Experimental results demonstrate improved combustion efficiency and a marked reduction in harmful emissions. The system is cost-effective, environmentally sustainable, and operable without grid electricity, making it an ideal solution for off-grid applications.
